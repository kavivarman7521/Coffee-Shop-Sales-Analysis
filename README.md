# Coffee-Shop-Sales-Analysis
Coffee Shop Sales Performance & Consumer Behavior Analysis
1. Project Overview
This project involves a comprehensive analysis of sales data for a coffee shop chain operating across multiple locations (Astoria, Hell’s Kitchen, and Lower Manhattan). The goal was to transform raw transactional data into an interactive dashboard to identify peak sales periods, popular product categories, and store performance metrics to drive data-informed business decisions.
2. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
The dashboard tracks four primary metrics to provide an immediate health check of the business:
Total Sales: $698,812.33
Total Footfall: 149,116 customers
Average Bill per Person: $4.69
Average Order per Person: 1.44 items
3. Key Insights & Findings
Peak Operating Hours: Quantity ordered peaks significantly between 8:00 AM and 10:00 AM, indicating a heavy morning rush. Sales remain steady throughout the afternoon before dropping sharply after 7:00 PM.
Product Mix: Coffee (39%) and Tea (28%) are the primary revenue drivers, accounting for 67% of total sales. Bakery items follow at 12%.
Top Products: "Barista Espresso" is the highest-selling product ($91,406), followed by "Brewed Chai Tea" and "Hot Chocolate."
Location Performance: Hell’s Kitchen is the top-performing location in terms of both sales ($236,511) and footfall, closely followed by Astoria and Lower Manhattan.
Daily Trends: Sales are relatively consistent throughout the week, with Friday seeing the highest volume of orders (21,701) and Saturday seeing the lowest (20,510), suggesting a stronger weekday commuter customer base.
Size Preferences: Customers prefer "Regular," "Large," and "Not Defined" sizes almost equally (~30% each), with "Small" being the least popular (9%).
4. Technical Tools Used
Data Cleaning & Transformation: Power Query (Excel) to handle missing values and format date/time fields.
Data Analysis: Pivot Tables for aggregating sales by hour, location, and category.
Data Visualization: Excel Dashboard featuring:
Slicers: Interactive filters for Month and Day to allow for granular time-series analysis.
Line Charts: To visualize hourly footfall trends.
Pie/Donut Charts: For categorical distribution.
Bar/Column Charts: For comparative analysis of locations and products.
5. Strategic Recommendations
Staffing Optimization: Increase staff density during the 8:00 AM – 10:00 AM window to reduce wait times and maximize throughput.
Upselling Opportunities: Given the low average bill of $4.69, implement "bundle deals" (e.g., Coffee + Bakery item) to increase the average transaction value.
Targeted Marketing: Since tea accounts for a large portion of sales (28%), consider expanding the premium tea selection or seasonal tea-based drinks.
Inventory Management: Align inventory orders for the "Barista Espresso" and "Brewed Chai Tea" to ensure no stock-outs of high-demand items during peak hours.
